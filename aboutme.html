About Me

I am a bioinformatician with a PhD in Evolutionary Genomics, an MSc in Bioinformatics, and a BSc in Microbiology. I develop and apply reproducible computational workflows that translate complex biological data into robust, interpretable results.

My research integrates high-throughput omics, including RNA-seq, metagenomics, metatranscriptomics, and metabolomics, with rigorous computational and statistical methods. I work at the interface of comparative genomics and transcriptomics, integrative multi-omics, and machine learning based modelling, with a strong emphasis on reproducibility, scalability, and transparent reporting.

In my PhD research, I investigate the dynamics of chromosome evolution in termites, focusing on the chromosomal chains that males of many species form during meiosis. I study how and why these meiotic chains arise and whether they are associated with elevated rates of structural genome rearrangements, using comparative genomics, transcriptomics, and machine-learning approaches.

I am committed to collaborative research and training. I have served as a bioinformatics trainer and workshop instructor, supported collaborators with workflow development and analysis, and contributed to research supervision and manuscript preparation.
